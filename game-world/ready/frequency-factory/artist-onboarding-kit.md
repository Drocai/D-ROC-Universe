### **PART 1: PREPARING YOUR SUBMISSION**

#### **Before You Submit**

**1. Is Your Track Ready?**

‚úÖ Professional Quality Checklist:
- [ ] Mix sounds clean (no mud, clipping, or harshness)
- [ ] Mastered to streaming standards (-14 LUFS)
- [ ] Vocals sit properly in mix
- [ ] Bass is controlled
- [ ] High end is crisp but not harsh

‚úÖ Creative Quality Checklist:
- [ ] Hook is memorable
- [ ] Arrangement keeps listener engaged
- [ ] Lyrics are clear and meaningful
- [ ] Performance is confident
- [ ] Track has a unique element
- [ ] You're genuinely proud of it

**If you answered NO to more than 2 items, consider revising before submitting.**

**2. Gather Your Materials**

üìù **Required:**
- Audio file (MP3/WAV) OR streaming link (Spotify/Apple Music)
- Track title
- Artist name
- Genre
- Cover art (square, min 1000x1000px)

üìù **Optional but Recommended:**

**3. Create Killer Cover Art**

Your cover art is the FIRST thing people see. Make it count.

**Do:**
- Use high-resolution images (min 3000x3000px)
- Keep text readable at small sizes
- Match the vibe of your music
- Be original and eye-catching
- Use professional design (or Canva)

**Don't:**
- Use low-quality images
- Overcomplicate with too many elements
- Use copyrighted images without permission

**Tools:**

**Bad Description Example:**

**Format:**
- 2-3 sentences max
- Mention influences/inspirations

- Include any interesting story

### **PART 3: HANDLING FEEDBACK**

#### **The Right Mindset**

**Remember:**
- Ratings are OPINIONS, not facts
- Even hit songs get mixed reviews
- Constructive criticism helps you grow
- One low rating doesn't define you
- Focus on patterns, not outliers

---

#### **Types of Feedback**

**1. Constructive Criticism**
```
Example: "Love the energy, but vocals are buried in the mix"
Your Response: Thank them, consider the feedback
```

**2. Harsh but Honest**
```
Example: "Flow is off-beat, lyrics are cliche"
Your Response: Don't take it personally, assess if it's valid
```

**3. Personal Attacks**
```
Example: "You should quit music"
Your Response: Report if violates guidelines, ignore otherwise
```

**4. Generic Praise**
```
Example: "This is fire! üî•"
Your Response: Appreciate it, but don't put too much weight on it
```

**5. Detailed Analysis**
```
Example: "Production is clean. Hook at 0:45 is catchy. Consider bringing vocals up 2dB in verse 2. Overall solid track!"
Your Response: GOLD. This is what you want. Take notes.
```

---

#### **What to Do with Feedback**

**Look for Patterns:**
- 3+ people mention same issue? ‚Üí Probably valid
- Split opinions? ‚Üí Stylistic choice, your call
- Outlier criticism? ‚Üí Consider but don't obsess

**Action Steps:**
1. **Read all feedback** (don't cherry-pick)
2. **Identify patterns** (common themes)
3. **Separate emotion from analysis** (what's actually being said?)
4. **Make a list** (actionable improvements)
5. **Implement learnings** (in next release)

**Example:**
```
Feedback Received:
- "Vocals too quiet" (5 mentions)
- "Hook is weak" (3 mentions)
- "Beat is fire" (8 mentions)

Action Items for Next Track:
‚úì Work with mix engineer on vocal levels
‚úì Spend more time on hook writing
‚úì Keep working with same producer
```

---

#### **How to Respond to Feedback**

**You CAN:**
- Reply to comments with gratitude
- Ask clarifying questions
- Engage constructively
- Thank reviewers

**You CAN'T:**
- Argue with raters
- Demand higher scores
- Harass users
- Get defensive

**Good Responses:**
‚úÖ "Thanks for the feedback! I'll work on that vocal mix."
‚úÖ "Appreciate you taking the time. What didn't work about the hook?"
‚úÖ "Glad you enjoyed it! Any specific part stand out?"

**Bad Responses:**
‚ùå "You don't understand my artistic vision"
‚ùå "Your rating is wrong"
‚ùå "Listen again and change your score"

#### **Industry Terms to Know**

**A&R** - Artists & Repertoire (talent scout)
**BPM** - Beats Per Minute (tempo)
**DAW** - Digital Audio Workstation (production software)
**Mix** - Balancing all elements (EQ, compression, effects)
**Master** - Final polish for streaming/distribution
**Stems** - Individual track elements (vocal, drums, bass, etc.)
**Demo** - Rough version of song
**Reference Track** - Song to model production after
**LUFS** - Loudness Units Full Scale (mastering standard)
**808** - Bass drum sound (from Roland TR-808)
